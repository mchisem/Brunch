Boss Bitch of the Month


So you work nonprofit for in Long Beach, what are some things you love about your job?
Working for a nonprofit has allowed me to give back to my community, network with industry leaders, and help make a change in the lives of children and families in Long Beach and L.A. I love being able to see the transformation of a child that walks through the door, from the beginning of their treatment to the end. This makes me feel that all the work we do is actually worth something.

What are some ways you combat the pressures of raising donations? Or better yet, how do you find the strength to ask for donations?
Asking for donations isn’t easy for me. The question I use to ask myself is, “How am I suppose to make people want to give me their hard earned money.” Especially when I was tasked with bringing in new millennial donors. Planning ahead of time always helps with the stress of finding donors. If you have a plan and a campaign strategy then executing your goals becomes much simpler. If your message is honest, and you are showing people exactly how their money can help benefit the lives of children then they’re more inclined to give. Telling a real story helps alleviate pressure as well because it resonates with the right people. I find the strength to ask for donations by remembering who and what I am raising money for. They’re kids that come in our organization in winter without jackets, holes in their shoes, and are freezing cold. That’s what gives me the strength.

You’re such a supportive person! In what ways do you support yourself?
When you work in the mental health field even in the administration department it can be a very heavy task on your heart and with your workload. I make sure to take time to myself everyday. I meditate for 15 mins to center my mind and thoughts. Hearing stories about victims of abuse especially children can stay with you, so it is important to take time to relax and quiet your mind. I also make sure to spend time with family and friends who are supportive.

What are some long term goals for you? Does it involve continuing to work for a nonprofit, do you have any projects you want to do or that you’re working on?
My long term goal is to own my own business where I am able to be creative, strategize and help others grow their business. I want to make an impact in this world. This is just the beginning for me.

So I kind of want to make this into a bit of a referral situation. Is there any woman that you admire or that you think would be suited for Boss Bitch of the Month?
Besides you? lol My friend Monae. She has several jobs, streams of income, and is a boss ass bitch!
